Product Features


  • Monitor

  • Alert

Monitor

OpenALPR constantly monitors your video streams and records every license plate that it sees.

The GUI is updated in near real-time, so you can view results on the Dashboard as plates are detected.

The license plate data is organized by site and by camera. You can choose to view all plates results or sort on a particular location. Once you've found a plate of interest, you can review a thumbnail image of the plate and overview view image of the vehicle to visually verify that the OpenALPR result.

Alert

Enter a list of license plates that you want to trigger alerts. OpenALPR compares each plate that it sees to your list. If there is a match, we'll send you an e-mail with license plate details for your review.

Each alert is saved in the system for at least 2 years. You can search over lengthy historic periods and find out when a particular license plate was seen.

Search

All license plate data is stored in the OpenALPR cloud. If you want to find a particular license plate, it's easy.

Search the database using any of the following properties:

  • Type (Plate, Plate Group, or Alert)
  • Plate Number
  • Date/Time
  • Site

After searching, you are provided with a detailed list of every license plate that matched your search.

Integrate

Integrate license plate analytics into your products and services. OpenALPR's License Plate Recognition platform exposes a number of APIs to make the data available to your systems.

Contact Us to discuss the best approach for adding license plate recognition into your application.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the AGPL and Commercial license?

OpenALPR provides its software under a dual license model to meet the development and distribution needs of both commercial customers as well as open source projects. The GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL) is an open source, copyleft software license.

For open-source projects, you may use the software under the terms of the AGPL. One of the requirements of this license is that your software must also be released under the same AGPL open source license.

For commercial customers, we offer a commercial license that supersedes the AGPL and allows OpenALPR to be linked into closed-source, proprietary applications. Additionally, commercial customers have access to a number of exciting enhancements that improve license plate recognition performance and efficiency. Please contact us for more information about our commercial licensing options.

What are the commercial enhancements?

The core library used to process license plates is the same in the open source and commercial versions. However, the commercial version includes additional proprietary algorithms that enhance the efficiency when monitoring streaming video. Specifically, the commercial version of OpenALPR is able to:

  1. Analyze video more efficiently. Rather than analyzing all pixels for each frame of the video, OpenALPR detects vehicles in the stream and only targets those for plate detection.
  2. Utilize multiple CPU cores simultaneously. You can configure the number of CPU cores that you wish to use for license plate analysis. This provides a linear increase in the ALPR processing rate.
  3. Process plates during quiet periods. OpenALPR buffers images during busy periods to process when the activity level is lower. This gives you more reads on each vehicle without having to increase CPU resources.
  4. Group plate results together. OpenALPR tracks a vehicle as it moves across the camera and combines all ALPR recognition events into a single entry for that vehicle. The plate number for the group uses the individual result with the highest confidence.

Do I get the commercial enhancements when I sign up for OpenALPR Cloud?

Yes. The OpenALPR Cloud agent includes all of the commercial enhancements to provide you with the best possible ALPR performance. The commercial version utilizes your CPU resources more efficiently, processes the plate images faster, and produces valuable plate group data.

How does OpenALPR Cloud work?

The OpenALPR Cloud collects metadata (plate numbers, confidence percentages, timestamps, etc.) from an OpenALPR Agent installed in your network. OpenALPR Cloud makes this information available for you to browse and search. It also sends e-mail alerts when plates are identified that match your alert configuration.

As shown in the diagram above, the OpenALPR Agent connects directly to your IP camera(s) and processes the video to identify license plates. The images that contain plates are stored locally on the agent in a rolling buffer. The size of this buffer is configurable based on available disk storage space. While reviewing license plate metadata on OpenALPR Cloud, your browser requests image data directly from the agent.

How is data secured in OpenALPR Cloud?

We use a variety of industry-standard security technologies and procedures to help protect your license plate data and personal data from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. When you enter sensitive information (such as a credit card number) we encrypt the transmission of that information using secure socket layer technology (SSL). We do not store your credit card information. Rather, we partner with a certified PCI Service Provider Level 1 merchant gateway. This is the most stringent level of credit card security certification available.

License plate data is similarly encrypted with SSL when sent to OpenALPR. The license plate images are persisted on your OpenALPR Agent systems. These images can be served within your network and do not need to be transferred over the internet to be displayed. The license plate data is stored on password-protected database systems that are kept isolated in a private network.

We also require you to enter a password to access your account information. Please do not disclose your account password to unauthorized people. Despite these measures, you should know that OpenALPR cannot fully eliminate security risks associated with license plate data and personal data and mistakes and security breaches may happen. If you have any questions or concerns about security, please contact us.

How am I billed for the cloud service?

Your first 30 days are on us. No credit card is required to try it out! If you love OpenALPR Cloud after your initial free trial (which we know you will), just enter your credit card into the system and billing will begin automatically on the 28th of each month. You may also pay by invoice if you prefer. Contact us to get an account setup.

OpenALPR Cloud charges you a pro-rated daily amount based on the number of cameras that you are actively using. For example, if you used 4 cameras for the first half of the month, and then increased to 6 cameras for the second half of the month, your bill for the month would be $250 -- (4 cameras * one month of service) + (2 cameras * half a month of service)

Can I embed OpenALPR into my proprietary application?

Absolutely. OpenALPR offers commercial licenses that you may purchase for this purpose. The OpenALPR library is directly compatible with C/C++, C#, Java, and Python code. Applications written in other languages can also integrate with the software via web service.

What countries do you support?

OpenALPR includes support for a number of countries. The software includes trained data for North American style and European style plates. These styles work well for a number of countries that use similar looking plates but may not necessarily be in that region. For countries with plates that look significantly different, OpenALPR can be trained to recognize them. We offer training services as a paid consulting engagement. You may also train the software yourself.

What cameras work best with the software?

OpenALPR will work with any camera that supports MJPEG streams. This includes visible-light and infrared cameras. The camera and optics should be configured such that the license plates are clearly legible in the video stream.

Once I enroll, what happens next?

After you login to OpenALPR Cloud, there is documentation under the "Getting Started" link. This walks you through the process of downloading and configuring an OpenALPR agent to start processing license plates.

What if I have technical questions?

The answer to your question may be found on the developer forum. You can also contact us directly with your questions.
