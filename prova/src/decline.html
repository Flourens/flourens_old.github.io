Stages of sexual function

Sex is an important part of every man’s life. But, it’s more than just a pleasurable activity and means of procreation. It affects mental health, interpersonal relationships, and a variety of other health areas. Men need to take care of their sexual health in order to engage and reap the benefits.

Every man needs to possess the three sexual health markers in order to participate in sex: sex drive (libido), arousal quality (erections) and prostate function (climax). These markers can become weaker with age, lifestyle factors, and progression through the three stages of sexual function.

When any or all of the three sexual health markers start functioning at less than healthy, optimal levels, it could be a sign of sexual decline.

The symptoms of sexual decline can be not only embarrassing, they can also be indicative of an underlying health issue that needs to be addressed.

What causes sexual decline symptoms?

When bio-drivers aren’t functioning at their peaks, the physical symptoms of sexual decline occur. Reduction of these bio-drivers, which leads to the breakdown of the three sexual health markers, can be caused by a variety of age and lifestyle factors. These can include stress, smoking, diet, quality of sleep, medical conditions, and side effects of certain medications.

The downward spiral of SD.

Causes
Age, smoking, lifestyle factors, and medical conditions.
Effects
Reduction of bio-drivers testosterone, nitric oxide, blood flow, and prostate function.
Consequences
Breakdown of sexual health markers libido, arousal qual ity, and prostate health.
Outcomes
Decline in sexual function, strength, performance, and enjoyment.

Testosterone levels drop
A decreased sex drive can be caused by low levels of testosterone, which can go down 2-4% every year after age 35 based on lifestyle habits and overall health.

Nitric oxide production decrease and blood flow slows
By age 40, men produce up to 55% less nitric oxide than they did in their prime, which leads to problems achieving and/or maintaining erections. This may be due to a number of factors, including certain lifestyle risks (smoking, alcohol abuse and other drug use), as well as other medical conditions (diabetes, high blood pressure, and cholesterol or lipid problems).

The prostate swells
50% of men over 50 have a swollen prostate which puts pressure on the urethra that can make climax difficult to achieve, feel weak, incomplete, or unsatisfying.
