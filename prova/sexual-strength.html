What is Sexual Strength?

In clinical terms, a man's sexual strength is determined by the combined quality of the three key sexual health markers:

  • Libido (sex drive)
  • Arousal quality (erection)
  • Prostate health (climax).

Each marker is supported by an essential biological factor, or bio-driver.

Testosterone fuels a man's libido and is produced in the testes. Healthy levels of testosterone play an integral role in sexual drive, desire, arousal, and response. Studies show that a testosterone boost can benefit sexual health and performance.

Nitric Oxide and Blood Flow. Arousal quality and erection hardness depends on the ability of arteries to allow an inflow of blood throughout the pelvic floor and penile tissues. Nitric oxide is the chief mediator that causes the blood vessels to dilate and accommodate more oxygenated blood.

The prostate gland is a donut-shaped male reproductive gland that surrounds the urethra and is located just below the bladder. A healthy, non-swollen prostate is essential for a man to reach a strong, complete and satisfying climax. It’s also critical to overall sexual and urinary function.
